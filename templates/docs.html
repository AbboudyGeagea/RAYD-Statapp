Report 22

Flow Analytics & Physician Churn

Tracks how studies flow from referring physicians through modalities to specific AE devices and procedures. Surfaces referral trends, demographic patterns, and physician churn signals.

Tree Modality → AE → Procedure Flow

What it shows: A hierarchical tree that starts at the modality level (e.g. CT, MRI), branches into specific AE devices (e.g. CT_SCANNER_1), and terminates at the top 5 procedure codes performed on each device.

How it works: Groups studies by modality → storing_ae → procedure_code, counts occurrences, and limits each device branch to its top 5 procedures by volume.

Added value: Instantly reveals which devices are doing the most work and which procedures dominate each machine — critical for maintenance scheduling, protocol standardisation, and cross-device comparison.

Bar Top Referring Physicians — Study Volume

What it shows: Horizontal bar chart of the top 10 referring physicians ranked by total study count within the selected date range.

How it works: Counts studies grouped by physician name, excludes "Unknown", sorts descending, takes top 10.

Added value: Identifies your most productive referral relationships so business development can prioritise retention and relationship management.

Bar Physician Loyalty — Unique Patient Reach

What it shows: Top 10 physicians ranked by the number of distinct patients they referred — a breadth metric as opposed to raw volume.

How it works: Counts DISTINCT patient_db_uid per physician. A physician who sends the same 10 patients 100 times scores lower than one who sends 100 different patients once.

Added value: Distinguishes high-volume chronic-care physicians from broad-network community referrers — two very different business profiles requiring different engagement strategies.

Stacked Bar Physician → Modality Preference

What it shows: Stacked bars for the top 10 physicians where each colour segment represents a modality (CT, MRI, X-Ray, etc.). Reveals each physician's imaging mix.

How it works: Pivots the physician × modality study count matrix. Each bar's total equals that physician's volume; segments show the modality split.

Added value: Helps predict equipment demand when specific physician volumes shift, and identifies cross-selling opportunities (e.g. a CT-heavy physician who rarely orders MRI).

Bar Average Patient Age per Physician

What it shows: Average age of patients referred by each of the top 15 physicians (minimum 5 studies). A red reference line marks the global average across all studies.

How it works: Computes ROUND(AVG(age_at_exam)::numeric, 1) per physician, filters to those with ≥ 5 studies, sorts by volume and takes top 15. The global baseline is calculated across the entire filtered dataset.

Added value: Identifies physicians with unusually young or old patient panels — important for insurance billing mix, contrast protocol planning, and paediatric service capacity.

Bar Table Average Patient Age per Procedure Code

What it shows: Average patient age for the top 20 procedure codes by volume (bar chart), plus a full searchable table covering all procedure codes.

How it works: Groups by procedure_code, computes AVG age and COUNT, sorts by count descending. The table is client-side searchable.

Added value: Reveals age targeting of specific procedures — e.g. screening mammography should cluster around 40–70 years. Outliers may indicate protocol misuse or miscoding.

Stacked Bar Study Status per Top Physician

What it shows: For each of the top 10 physicians, a stacked bar breaks down their studies by workflow status (Completed, Cancelled, Pending, etc.).

How it works: Groups by physician × study_status (COALESCE to "Unknown"), counts studies, pivots into stacked series. Limited to physicians with known names.

Added value: High cancellation or pending rates for a specific physician may indicate scheduling friction, poor patient compliance, or order quality issues — all correctable with targeted outreach.

Bar Overall Case Status Distribution

What it shows: A bar chart of all study statuses across the entire filtered dataset — Completed, Cancelled, In-Progress, etc.

How it works: Simple value_counts on study_status from the base CTE filtered by date range and user-selected filters.

Added value: Workflow health indicator. A rising cancellation bar or shrinking completion bar is an early warning of operational disruption.

Rose Pie Gender Distribution

What it shows: A nightingale rose chart showing the proportion of studies by patient gender (Male / Female / Unknown).

How it works: Counts studies per gender value from patient demographics, renders as ECharts rose-type pie where petal radius encodes count.

Added value: Gender-specific service utilisation patterns. A significant skew may indicate a service gap (e.g. low female mammography uptake) or demographic specialty.

Area Line Age Group Distribution (Wave)

What it shows: A smooth area line chart across 7 age bands: Neonate (0–1m), Infant (1m–1y), Child (1–12y), Adolescent (12–18y), Young Adult (18–35y), Adult (35–64y), Senior (65+y).

How it works: Uses pandas pd.cut() with predefined bin edges to classify each patient's age_at_exam into a named bucket, then counts per bucket.

Added value: Population stratification for resource planning — paediatric suites, sedation protocols, and geriatric-friendly facilities all depend on knowing the age makeup of your patient base.

KPI Cards Physician Churn Alerts

What it shows: Alert cards for physicians whose referral volume declined month-over-month in the last 60 days. Shows current month vs prior month count and the percentage drop.

How it works: Compares two rolling 30-day windows (days 0–30 and days 30–60 before today). Any physician with a strictly lower current count triggers an alert card.

Added value: This is the platform's proactive retention tool. Catching a physician's drift before they stop referring entirely gives you a window to re-engage before revenue is permanently lost.

Report 23

Population Intelligence

Deep demographic analysis of the patient population using age, gender, patient class, and modality as dimensions. Designed for capacity planning, service mix decisions, and population health strategy.

Bar Patient Class Volume

What it shows: Study volume per patient class (Inpatient, Outpatient, Emergency, etc.).

How it works: Counts studies per patient_class, sorted by volume.

Added value: Inpatient vs outpatient split directly drives staffing, contrast preparation, and porter logistics. An ER spike visible here correlates with patient arrival patterns and may justify extended-hours planning.

Bar Average Age by Modality

What it shows: Mean patient age per modality with a reference line for the global average.

How it works: AVG(age_at_exam) grouped by modality, cast to numeric for rounding precision.

Added value: Bone density (DEXA) and echocardiography skew older; paediatric modalities skew younger. Mismatches between expected and actual age profiles may signal protocol or referral pathway issues.

Stacked Bar Age Group × Procedure Distribution

What it shows: For each of the 7 age bands, a stacked bar shows volume split across the top 5 procedure codes.

How it works: Pivot table of age_bucket × procedure_code, limited to top-5 procedures globally to keep the chart readable.

Added value: Shows whether procedure demand is age-driven (e.g. head CTs dominate in seniors) — essential for protocol development and paediatric subspecialty justification.

Rose Pie Gender Ratio

What it shows: Nightingale rose of M/F/Unknown proportions across the entire patient population within the filter window.

Added value: Baseline gender split for equity reporting and specialty planning (e.g. breast imaging capacity relative to female population share).

Area Line Monthly Volume Trend

What it shows: Total studies per calendar month as a smooth area line, revealing seasonal patterns.

How it works: DATE_TRUNC('month', study_date) groupby with count, ordered chronologically.

Added value: Seasonal capacity planning — summer dips, Ramadan shifts, and post-holiday surges are all visible here. Allows proactive staffing adjustments months in advance.

Heatmap Modality × Patient Class Heatmap

What it shows: A grid where rows are modalities, columns are patient classes, and cell colour intensity encodes the study count at each intersection.

How it works: Pivot table of modality × patient_class study counts. Colour scale from 0 to max cell value using ECharts visualMap.

Added value: Reveals demand concentration — e.g. if ER patients overwhelmingly use one modality, that device is a critical bottleneck for emergency care. Guides equipment prioritisation during incidents.

Stacked Bar Age Distribution per Modality

What it shows: For each modality, a stacked bar shows the breakdown across the 7 age bands (Neonate through Senior).

How it works: pd.cut() age bucketing followed by a modality × age_bucket pivot with fill_value=0. Each modality bar is normalised within itself via percentage mode.

Added value: Differentiates adult-centric vs paediatric-centric devices. A modality with 30% paediatric load needs different protocols, contrast doses, and staff training than one at 5%.

Multi-Axis Bar Age × Gender × Modality Cube

What it shows: Three-dimensional demographic breakdown: age groups on the X axis, study count on the Y axis, with stacked segments for gender and colour grouping by modality.

How it works: Three-level groupby (age_bucket × sex × modality) producing a nested structure rendered as multi-series stacked ECharts bars.

Added value: The most information-dense chart in the report. It can answer questions like "What is the volume of CT studies in adult females?" in a single view — critical for targeted health programmes and grant reporting.

KPI Cards Patient Return Rates (30 / 60 / 90 Days)

What it shows: Three KPI cards showing the percentage of patients who returned for a second visit within 30, 60, and 90 days of their first study in the period.

How it works: For each window, counts patients with ≥ 2 distinct study dates within that interval divided by total unique patients. Computed in SQL with HAVING COUNT(*) ≥ 2 and date difference conditions.

Added value: Patient loyalty and chronic disease burden indicator. High 30-day return rates may suggest follow-up imaging protocols are working; very high rates may indicate incomplete studies or equipment quality issues.

Report 25

Operations & Performance Excellence

The operational performance hub. Tracks turnaround time (TAT), equipment utilisation, RVU (revenue value units), and radiologist productivity. The most actionable report for radiology managers.

KPI Row Global Summary KPIs

What it shows: Five headline metrics: Global Utilisation %, Total RVU, High-Stress Devices, Active Scan Time, and ER Average TAT.

How it works: Each KPI is derived from the aggregated study dataset joined with the device schedule table. Utilisation = actual scan minutes ÷ scheduled capacity minutes × 100. RVU is summed from the procedure_duration_map rvu_value column. High-stress threshold is > 85% utilisation.

Added value: Gives the department head a 10-second read of operational health before drilling into detail. Red numbers demand attention; green numbers confirm normalcy.

Bar TAT per Patient Class

What it shows: Average total turnaround time (in minutes) broken down by patient class (ER, Inpatient, Outpatient, etc.).

How it works: TAT is calculated as the difference between study_date and report completion date in minutes. Grouped by patient_class, averaged, and sorted.

Added value: ER patients demand sub-60-minute TAT; inpatients can tolerate longer. This chart immediately flags SLA breaches by care setting, enabling targeted workflow interventions.

Bar Hourly Arrival Pattern

What it shows: Study arrival count by hour of day (00:00 – 23:00), averaged across the selected date range.

How it works: EXTRACT(HOUR FROM study_date) groupby with count, producing a 24-point histogram.

Added value: The staffing chart. If your peak hour is 10:00–12:00 and your second radiologist starts at 12:00, you now have evidence to shift that schedule. Also reveals night-shift demand for on-call planning.

Pie Modality Volume Split

What it shows: Percentage of total studies attributed to each modality as a donut chart.

Added value: Equipment investment context. If CT is 60% of volume but only 20% of revenue (RVU), the resource allocation story needs revisiting.

Scatter Duration vs TAT Correlation

What it shows: A scatter plot where each dot is a procedure code. X axis = average scan duration (minutes), Y axis = average total TAT (minutes).

How it works: Groups by procedure_code, computes mean(scan_duration) and mean(total_tat_min). Each point is labelled with the procedure code on hover.

Added value: Points far above the diagonal have long TATs relative to their scan time — the reporting bottleneck is after the scan. Points near the diagonal have proportionate TAT. Outliers are where to focus workflow improvement.

Matrix Table Device Utilisation & Revenue Matrix

What it shows: A heat-coloured table with AE devices as rows and weekdays as columns. Each cell shows utilisation % for that device on that day of the week, plus a total RVU column.

How it works: Joins studies with device_weekly_schedule and device_exceptions for actual vs scheduled capacity. Colour coding: green (normal), amber (>75%), red (>85% = high stress), grey (closed/no schedule).

Added value: The most comprehensive equipment performance view. Identifies devices that are chronically overloaded on specific days (schedule redistribution target) and devices that are consistently idle (potential decommissioning or redeployment candidates).

Bar Best vs Worst AE by Average TAT

What it shows: All AE devices ranked by their average TAT from best (left) to worst (right), filtered to devices with ≥ 5 studies for statistical reliability.

How it works: Groups by aetitle, computes mean(total_tat_min), filters count ≥ 5, sorts ascending.

Added value: Surface-level anomaly detection for equipment or workflow issues. A device with a dramatically higher TAT might have a worklist configuration problem, network latency, or a staffing issue on its floor.

Bar TAT Distribution Histogram

What it shows: Frequency histogram of TAT values bucketed into 8 bands: 0–30 min, 31–60 min, 61–120 min, 121–180 min, 181–240 min, 241–300 min, 301–360 min, and 360+ min.

How it works: Filters studies with TAT > 0, bins using pandas pd.cut(), counts per bin. A reference line marks the global mean TAT.

Added value: Reveals TAT variability. A narrow tall distribution = consistent performance. A flat wide distribution = high variability needing standardisation. A bimodal shape = two distinct workflow streams (e.g. daytime vs overnight reads).

Scatter RVU vs TAT Correlation

What it shows: Each study as a dot: X = TAT in minutes, Y = RVU value. Reveals whether high-value procedures receive proportionately fast or slow turnaround.

How it works: Joins studies with procedure_duration_map for RVU values. Filters out zero-TAT and zero-RVU records. Samples up to 2,000 points for performance.

Added value: High-RVU studies with long TATs represent the most critical improvement targets — revenue is being generated but delayed, potentially impacting referring physician satisfaction and repeat business.

Table TAT Outlier Detection Table

What it shows: Studies whose TAT exceeded 2× the global mean TAT, shown in a sortable table with study date, modality, procedure, AE device, and actual TAT.

How it works: Filters df[df['total_tat_min'] > 2 × global_mean_tat], sorts by TAT descending, shows top 50.

Added value: The action list. These are specific studies that experienced severe delays. Each row is a candidate for root-cause analysis — was the radiologist absent? Was the worklist stuck? Was it a holiday? This table turns a statistical observation into an auditable case file.

Cards + Table Radiologist Performance Cards

What it shows: Per-radiologist cards showing total studies read, average TAT, and total RVU. Clicking a card drills into a table breaking down their performance by location and modality.

How it works: Groups by reporting physician (radiologist), computes count, AVG(TAT), SUM(RVU). Drill-down table further groups by location × modality.

Added value: Individual productivity transparency for management reviews, balanced workload assignment, and identifying training needs. The modality breakdown reveals where each radiologist spends their time vs their subspecialty.

Horizontal Bar Scatter Overlay Modality TAT Infra tab

What it shows: Average and median turnaround time (in minutes) per imaging modality, displayed as horizontal bars with a scatter overlay, sorted fastest to slowest. Only modalities with ≥ 5 signed studies are included.

How it works: Groups signed studies by modality, computes both mean and median TAT per group, excludes modalities below the minimum study threshold, and sorts ascending by average TAT.

Added value: Reveals which modalities (CT, MRI, etc.) have structurally longer reporting delays rather than isolated incidents. When mean and median diverge significantly for a modality, a small number of extreme outliers are pulling the average — a distinct problem from systematic slowness. Essential for staffing decisions and SLA target-setting per modality type.

KPI Cards Stacked Bar Unread Study Aging Workflow tab

What it shows: Unread studies bucketed by how long they have been waiting — 0–24 h, 24–48 h, 48–72 h, and 72 h+. KPI cards summarise each bucket's total count, and a stacked bar breaks the same buckets down by modality.

How it works: Filters to studies without a signed report, calculates elapsed time from study date to now, classifies each into an aging bucket, and pivots by modality for the stacked bar series.

Added value: The 72 h+ bucket is a red flag — studies waiting more than three days without a read represent a direct clinical risk and a potential SLA breach. The modality breakdown pinpoints which imaging types are accumulating a backlog, enabling targeted radiologist reassignment before the situation becomes a patient safety issue.

KPI Cards Donut Studies per Shift Workflow tab

What it shows: Study volume distributed across Morning, Afternoon, and Night shifts, displayed as KPI cards with totals and a donut chart showing the proportional split. Shift hours are configurable per hospital via the "Configure Shifts" button — these settings are stored permanently.

How it works: Study arrival times are classified into shifts using the hour boundaries defined in the hospital's shift configuration. Studies are then counted per shift and the results are normalised as percentages for the donut chart. Click "Configure Shifts" to set your hospital's actual shift boundaries.

Added value: Identifies load imbalance between shifts. If the Night shift is consistently absorbing disproportionate volume while Morning is underutilised, staffing rosters can be adjusted with data to back the decision. The configurable shift boundaries ensure the analysis reflects each hospital's real operational structure rather than a generic template.

KPI Horizontal Bar Addendum Rate by Radiologist Workflow tab

What it shows: The overall percentage of finalized reports that received an addendum (KPI), plus a horizontal bar chart breaking the addendum rate down per radiologist. Only radiologists with ≥ 5 finalized reports are included for statistical reliability.

How it works: Counts reports flagged as addended divided by total finalized reports, both globally and per radiologist. Radiologists below the minimum volume threshold are excluded. Results are sorted by addendum rate descending.

Added value: A high addendum rate for a specific radiologist may indicate errors, incomplete initial reads, or a consistently complex case mix. It is a quality indicator, not automatically a performance problem — context matters. Used alongside case volume and case complexity data, it supports constructive peer review conversations and helps distinguish systematic issues from individual case circumstances.

KPI Cards Bar Charts Flagged Table Technician Performance & TAT Technicians tab

What it shows: End-to-end technician turnaround time — the duration from when a patient exam is scheduled (or received via HL7) to when the technician marks it as "Done" on the Live AE Status page. Includes per-technician cards, procedure-level actual vs expected comparison, and a flagged exams table.

How it works: Joins hl7_orders (where done_at and done_by are set by the Live Feed "Done" button) with procedure_duration_map for expected durations. Start time uses scheduled_datetime with a fallback to received_at when no schedule exists. Each exam is flagged: too short (< 50% of expected duration) or too long (> 200% of expected duration).

Components:

  • Summary KPIs — total completed exams, active technicians, average & median TAT, too-short and too-long counts
  • Per-Technician Cards — name, exam count, avg/median TAT, modality breakdown, colour-coded flag bar (green = normal, amber = too short, red = too long)
  • Procedure Duration Chart — horizontal bar comparing average actual completion time vs expected duration per procedure code, colour-coded by deviation
  • Technician Comparison Chart — horizontal bar ranking all technicians by average TAT, colour-coded by flag ratio
  • Flagged Exams Table — scrollable table of abnormal exams with accession, procedure, modality, technician, actual vs expected time, and timestamp

Added value: The first objective measurement of technician efficiency in the department. "Too short" flags may indicate skipped protocol steps or premature completion clicks. "Too long" flags surface bottlenecks — patient delays, equipment issues, or training needs. The per-procedure comparison reveals which exam types consistently deviate from expectations, enabling targeted procedure duration recalibration or workflow review. Combined with radiologist TAT from the same report, provides a complete study lifecycle view.

Report 27

Audit & Period Comparison

Validates data integrity between the RIS (Radiology Information System) and PACS, and enables side-by-side comparison of two time periods to measure growth or regression.

Area Line Hourly Load — Volume Trend

What it shows: Order arrival volume grouped by hour of day, rendered as a gradient area line.

How it works: EXTRACT(HOUR FROM scheduled_datetime) from the HL7 orders table, counted by hour.

Added value: RIS-side order scheduling pattern — shows when orders are placed, not just when scans happen. Useful for identifying front-desk peak load and PACS worklist surge management.

Donut Procedure ID Reconciliation (RIS vs PACS)

What it shows: A donut chart showing the percentage of orders where the procedure code in the RIS matches the procedure code recorded in PACS vs those that are mismatched or unlinked.

How it works: Joins hl7_orders with etl_orders on accession number, compares procedure_code fields. Matches = identical; mismatches = different codes; orphans = no join found.

Added value: Data quality audit. A low match rate indicates protocol naming inconsistencies between RIS and PACS, which corrupts billing accuracy, procedure statistics, and AI model training. Target: > 95% match rate.

Stacked Bar Demographics — Age × Gender

What it shows: Age group distribution (X axis) with male/female stacking — a classic population pyramid in bar form.

Added value: Demographic signature of the order population — useful for comparing period A vs period B to see if patient composition has shifted.

Horizontal Bar Order Status Mix

What it shows: Order lifecycle status (Active, Cancelled, Completed, etc.) from the RIS as a horizontal bar chart.

Added value: Monitors order pipeline health from the RIS perspective. High "cancelled" volume may indicate scheduling inefficiency or patient no-show problems requiring a dedicated intervention.

Bar Modality Distribution by AE

What it shows: Order count per AE device, colour-coded by modality, showing which devices received the most orders from the RIS.

Added value: Cross-validates RIS order routing against PACS actual device usage. Discrepancies between this chart and Report 25's device matrix may reveal orders being re-routed or performed on unscheduled devices.

KPI Cards Audit KPIs: Match %, Orphans, Duration, Volume Delta

What it shows: Four headline numbers — RIS-PACS procedure code match rate (%), orphaned order count, average procedure duration, and the volume delta between period A and period B.

Added value: The volume delta answers the single most common management question: "Are we doing more or less than this time last year?" Paired with the match rate, it confirms whether the growth is real or an artefact of data quality issues.

Report 29

Infrastructure & Storage Audit

PACS storage consumption analysis by modality, device, and procedure. Designed for IT infrastructure planning and protocol optimisation to control archive growth.

Bar Storage Distribution by Modality (GB)

What it shows: Total archive storage in GB consumed by each modality (CT, MRI, X-Ray, etc.) within the selected period.

How it works: Aggregates image_size_kb from etl_image_locations joined with etl_didb_studies, grouped by modality, converted to GB.

Added value: CT and MRI are disproportionate storage consumers due to multi-slice protocols. This chart quantifies the imbalance and informs tiered-storage decisions (e.g. moving old CT to cold storage first).

Bar Device Storage Efficiency (Avg MB / Study)

What it shows: Average megabytes per study for each AE device. Bars above 500 MB are highlighted in red as storage hogs.

How it works: SUM(image_size_kb) ÷ COUNT(studies) per storing_ae, converted to MB. The 500 MB threshold is configurable in the route.

Added value: Identifies devices acquiring far more data per study than clinically necessary — often a protocol configuration issue (e.g. unnecessarily thin CT slices, over-scanned sequences). Fixing one protocol on one device can save tens of TB annually.

Table Protocol Density Inventory

What it shows: Full table of procedure_code × modality × storing_ae combinations with study count, average MB per study, and total GB. Sortable and exportable.

How it works: Three-level groupby from the storage summary table (summary_storage_daily), no row limit, paginated client-side.

Added value: The most granular storage view in the platform. IT and radiology physics teams can use this to create a ranked list of protocol optimisation targets — combining high volume × high MB/study for maximum impact per intervention.

Alert Cards Storage Hog Alerts

What it shows: Red alert cards for any procedure code averaging > 500 MB per study, showing the procedure code, average MB, and the AE device responsible.

Added value: Actionable alerts that turn a passive inventory into a to-do list for the PACS administrator. Each card is a physics meeting agenda item.

Report AI

Predictive Intelligence & Anomaly Detection

Machine-learning forecasting applied to storage, study volume, equipment utilisation, and physician referral trends. Uses linear regression and standard deviation–based anomaly detection. All models run on your server — no external API calls.

Line Storage Forecast — Days to Capacity

What it shows: Historical daily storage consumption (GB) as a solid line, with a 90-day linear regression forecast as a dashed projection. The KPI above the chart shows current total GB, daily growth rate, and estimated days until capacity is reached.

How it works: Aggregates daily archive growth from summary_storage_daily. Fits a linear regression model (numpy polyfit, degree 1) to the time series. Projects forward 90 days. Days-to-full = (capacity_gb − current_gb) ÷ daily_growth_gb.

Added value: Replaces gut-feel storage procurement with a data-driven runway estimate. Procurement cycles for NAS expansion are typically 8–12 weeks — this forecast ensures you never run out of storage unexpectedly.

Line + Anomaly Flags Volume Forecast & Anomaly Detection

What it shows: Daily study count history as a line, with a 90-day volume forecast and red/orange markers on days that were anomalous (statistical outliers). A separate pie shows the modality split of total volume.

How it works: Linear regression on the daily study count time series. Anomalies are flagged where the actual count deviates more than ±2 standard deviations from the rolling trend. YoY comparison uses the same calendar period from the previous year.

Added value: Anomaly flags on specific dates give you starting points for investigation — a volume spike on a holiday could be a data duplication error; a sudden drop might be a worklist failure that went unnoticed. The forecast supports budget planning and staffing contracts.

Line + Forecast Per-Device Utilisation Forecast

What it shows: For each AE device, a line chart of historical utilisation % (actual scan minutes ÷ scheduled capacity minutes) with a 30-day forecast. Devices are listed as selectable cards with stress classification (High / Normal / Low).

How it works: Joins studies with device_weekly_schedule and device_exceptions to compute daily utilisation per AE. Linear regression per device produces the forecast slope. High-stress threshold: > 85%. Low utilisation threshold: < 30%.

Added value: Predicts which device will hit maximum capacity first and when. A device trending upward at 80% utilisation will breach 85% in a calculable number of weeks — enough lead time to negotiate a maintenance contract pause or add session capacity.

Scatter Stress vs Output (Efficiency Frontier)

What it shows: Each AE device as a dot: X axis = utilisation %, Y axis = total RVU. The ideal device is in the top-right quadrant (high utilisation, high revenue).

Added value: Classifies devices into four quadrants — Star (high util, high RVU), Workhorse (high util, low RVU), Underperformer (low util, low RVU), and Cash Cow (low util, high RVU). Each quadrant demands a different management response.

Line + Forecast Table Physician Referral Trends & Forecast

What it shows: Monthly referral count for each of the top 20 physicians as a selectable line chart, with a 3-month linear forecast. The accompanying table ranks physicians by volume and shows their YoY change %, trend slope (Growing / Stable / Declining), and forecast.

How it works: Monthly aggregation per physician. Linear regression on the monthly series produces the slope. Slope > 0.5 = Growing; slope < −0.5 = Declining. YoY = (current period count − same period last year) ÷ last year × 100.

Added value: The most strategically important module for business development. A physician classified as Declining with a negative YoY is a retention risk. A physician classified as Growing is a relationship to invest in. The 3-month forecast gives the business development team a quantified opportunity pipeline.

AI Narrative Automated Insight Summaries

What it shows: For each module (Storage, Volume, Utilisation, Physician), a natural-language paragraph summarising the key finding, the trend direction, and any anomalies detected. Generated automatically every time the report runs.

How it works: Template-based text generation using Python f-strings populated with computed metrics (slope, anomaly count, days-to-full, churning physician names). No external LLM required — fully deterministic.

Added value: Translates statistical results into plain English suitable for clinical leadership who may not interpret charts intuitively. The summary can be copied directly into a weekly operational report or email briefing.

Report Intelligence

Report Intelligence — NLP Analysis

Natural-language processing of radiology reports stored in the RIS. Analyses report text to extract clinical language patterns, classify outcomes, flag critical findings, and surface quality gaps — all without sending data outside your server.

Donut Treemap Normal / Abnormal Classification

What it shows: A donut chart splitting all analysed reports into Normal, Abnormal, and Critical categories. The treemap breaks down the same classification by modality, so you can immediately see which imaging type produces the highest abnormality rate.

How it works: Keyword-matching on report conclusion sections using curated term lists (e.g. "no acute", "unremarkable", "within normal limits" → Normal; "lesion", "mass", "fracture", "obstruction" → Abnormal; "critical", "urgent", "immediate" → Critical). Each report gets a single highest-severity classification.

Added value: A radiology department with a 40% abnormal rate in chest X-rays versus 15% in MSK is capturing a very different patient population. This baseline helps benchmark report quality and flag unexpected classification shifts that may indicate transcription shortcuts.

KPI Table Critical Findings Log

What it shows: A filterable table of every report flagged as Critical, showing accession number, patient ID, modality, radiologist, date, and the triggering phrase extracted from the report text.

How it works: Reports are scanned for a configurable list of critical-finding keywords. The exact sentence containing the trigger phrase is extracted and displayed. The list is exportable.

Added value: Provides an auditable critical-findings register without requiring any change to RIS workflow. Useful for accreditation evidence and for verifying that critical findings are being communicated in a timely manner.

Bar Section-Level Term Frequency

What it shows: Top N most frequent clinical terms broken down by report section: Technique, Findings (Résultats), and Conclusion. Each section is analysed independently, revealing vocabulary used at each stage of the reporting process.

How it works: Reports are parsed using section delimiters. Stop-words, conjunctions, and generic radiology boilerplate are removed. The remaining tokens are counted and ranked. Bigrams (two-word phrases) are also detected where frequency exceeds a threshold.

Added value: Identifies over-used phrases that may indicate template copying ("clinical correlation is suggested" appearing in 90% of conclusions) and helps standardise reporting language across the department.

Treemap Report Term Treemap

What it shows: A proportional treemap where each rectangle is a clinical term, sized by frequency and coloured by section origin (Technique / Findings / Conclusion). Hovering shows the exact count and percentage of reports containing that term.

Added value: Provides an immediate visual vocabulary fingerprint of the department. A department specialising in neuro will have a visually distinct treemap from one focused on MSK — deviations from the expected pattern may indicate data quality issues or unexpected case-mix shifts.

Table Bar Gap Analysis — Empty Sections per Radiologist

What it shows: For each radiologist, the percentage of reports where one or more structured sections (Technique, Findings, Conclusion) were left empty or contained fewer than a minimum character count. Presented as a ranked bar chart and sortable table.

How it works: Each report section is extracted and tested against a minimum-length threshold (configurable, default 20 characters). A report fails the gap check if any mandatory section is below the threshold. Rate = failed reports ÷ total reports for that radiologist.

Added value: Structural completeness is a core accreditation requirement. This module provides an objective, data-driven audit that can be used in radiologist performance reviews and for identifying training needs without manual report review.

Bar Physician Activity & Report Volume

Added value: Helps identify emerging diagnostic patterns — for example, a sudden cluster of pulmonary embolism-related reports over a two-week window that is not reflected in referral patterns could indicate a local outbreak or a shared environmental risk factor worth escalating.

AI

AI Assistant — Conversational Analytics

An on-premise anti-hallucination AI assistant powered by Llama 3.1 8B. Python fetches all facts from the database first; the model only converts structured facts into natural language. If no data is found, the model is skipped entirely. All conversations remain within your infrastructure — no data leaves the server.

Chat Conversational AI Interface

What it shows: A full-screen chat interface supporting Arabic and English. Responses include inline charts (pie, bar) and contextual links to relevant reports. A per-core CPU monitor shows model load in real time.

How it works: A five-stage anti-hallucination pipeline:

  1. Intent detection — Python matches question keywords against 12+ intent categories (modality, storage, TAT, physicians, orders, today, yesterday, weekly, monthly, trends, patient class, device utilisation)
  2. Fact retrieval — matched intents trigger SQL queries against PostgreSQL; results are formatted as plain-text facts with no table/column names exposed
  3. No-data guard — if no facts are found, the model is skipped entirely and a fixed message is returned
  4. Constrained inference — the model receives only the facts and a strict system prompt; temperature is set to 0.1 with a fixed seed for deterministic output
  5. Hallucination scan — a compiled regex checks the output for SQL keywords, table names, column names, and code blocks; any match triggers a fallback message

Performance: Base context (department overview + AE list) is cached for 60 seconds. Identical questions are served from a response cache (5-minute TTL, up to 200 entries) — zero inference cost on repeats.

Added value: The model never invents numbers, names, or dates. Every fact in the response is traceable to a specific SQL query. Non-technical staff get natural-language answers with charts and report links, while the anti-hallucination architecture ensures clinical data integrity. Because the model runs locally on CPU, there are no API costs and no data leaves the facility.

Feedback Teaching Feedback & Learning System

What it shows: Every AI response includes thumbs-up / thumbs-down buttons. End-users rate responses with a single click. Administrators access the AI Teaching Center (/ai/admin) to review thumbs-down feedback and teach corrections.

How it works: Feedback is stored in the ai_feedback table with the original question, response, vote, and user ID. Corrections are stored in ai_corrections with keyword triggers and the correct answer. When a new question matches a correction's keywords, the correction is injected as a few-shot example in the model's system prompt — the model learns without retraining.

Admin workflow:

  • Review thumbs-down entries in the Feedback tab
  • Click "Teach" to expand an inline form with auto-extracted keywords
  • Type the correct answer and save — the AI uses it immediately
  • Or click "Dismiss" to mark as reviewed without teaching
  • Manage all active corrections in the Corrections tab

Added value: The AI improves continuously without model retraining, GPU resources, or technical expertise. End-users contribute effortlessly (one click), and administrators make targeted corrections weekly. Each correction costs only ~50 extra tokens in the prompt — negligible overhead for significantly better answers over time.

Alerts Proactive Anomaly Detection

What it shows: Automated alerts for TAT spikes, storage growth anomalies, and device volume outliers — computed entirely via SQL with no AI inference.

How it works: Three detection methods run on each call to /ai/alerts:

  • TAT spike — compares this week's average TAT against a 4-week rolling baseline; alerts at ≥ 20% deviation
  • Storage growth — compares this week's storage ingestion (GB) against the prior week; alerts at ≥ 25% increase
  • Device outliers — flags AE devices whose this-week study count exceeds the prior 5-week average by more than 2 standard deviations

Added value: Pure data-driven alerting with zero hallucination risk — no model involved. Surfaces operational anomalies before they become visible in weekly review meetings.

Config Setup Requirements

Requirements: Enabled via BITNET_ENABLED=true. Requires llama-server binary and a Llama 3.1 8B Q4_K_M GGUF model file (~5 GB). Runs as a systemd service on the host; the container reaches it via the host IP on port 8081.

Minimum hardware: 8 GB RAM, 4 CPU cores. Inference takes 5–15 seconds per question on CPU. The response cache eliminates repeated inference for identical questions within 5 minutes.

Added value: Entirely air-gapped — no API costs, no data-sharing agreements, no internet dependency. Suitable for healthcare environments with strict data-sovereignty requirements.

What it shows: A card per active AE device showing: modality label, minutes used vs scheduled capacity for today, and a colour-coded utilisation percentage (green <60%, amber 60–85%, red >85%). An overall site utilisation percentage is shown at the top.
