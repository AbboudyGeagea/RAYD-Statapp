Welcome to RAYD

RAYD is a radiology analytics platform for Intermedic. Use the navigation on the left to find any page. This guide explains what each section does and how to act on what you see.

New here? Start with these four steps.

1

Live Feed — Open this every morning to confirm all scanners are sending data to PACS. A device missing from the feed means its connection dropped.

2

Operations & Performance — Check the Global KPIs and the Unread Study Aging panel. These tell you immediately if anything is behind today.

3

Referral Trends — Review the Physician Churn Alerts weekly. This catches physicians drifting away before the revenue impact becomes visible in monthly numbers.

4

Super Report — Use this for monthly management summaries. Save your filter configuration once so it reruns in a single click every month.

Live

Live Feed

A real-time stream of studies arriving from PACS as they happen. Auto-refreshes every 15 seconds — no manual reload needed.

Live Real-Time AE Activity Feed

Is a specific scanner actually sending studies right now — and did the last patient's scan arrive in PACS?

If an AE title you expect to see is absent from the feed, its connection to PACS may have dropped. Investigate before the worklist grows stale and a radiologist notices missing cases.

KPI Strip Today's Activity KPIs

How many studies have arrived today, how many in the last hour, and how many devices are currently active?

A low "last hour" count during peak hours is a warning signal — check whether the ETL pipeline ran recently and whether the PACS connection is healthy.

Action Button Technician "Done" Button

Which HL7 orders have been physically completed by a technician, and who completed them?

Clicking Done records the technician's name and exact timestamp. This data directly powers the Technician Performance tab in Operations & Performance — without it, technician TAT cannot be calculated.

Capacity

Capacity Ladder

Ranks all imaging devices from most-loaded to least-loaded in real time. Updates on every page load using today's completed studies vs each room's scheduled capacity.

Ranked Bar Gauges Device Load Ladder

Which rooms are overloaded right now, and which have capacity to absorb more patients today?

Redirect walk-in patients or same-day bookings to rooms in the green zone (below 60%). A room in the red zone (above 85%) should receive no additional bookings until its current session ends. Colour codes: green <60%, amber 60–85%, red >85%.

HL7

HL7 Orders

Displays inbound HL7 orders received from the RIS/HIS. Provides a human-readable view of order data alongside the raw HL7 message for troubleshooting — no SSH access required.

Table Raw Message Order List & HL7 Inspector

Did a specific order arrive from the RIS — and what did the original HL7 message actually contain?

If a patient's exam is on the scanner but not showing in PACS, search for their accession number here. A missing order or a message with format errors explains the gap. Click any row to read the parsed HL7 segments alongside the raw pipe-delimited message.

Referral Trends

Tracks how studies flow from referring physicians through modalities to specific devices and procedures. Surfaces referral volume, patient demographics by physician, and early churn signals.

Volume & Flow
Tree Modality → Device → Procedure Flow

Which procedures dominate each scanner, and which devices carry the heaviest workload?

A device where 80% of volume is concentrated on one procedure is a single point of failure — if it goes down, all those procedures stop. Use this to diversify device utilisation and standardise protocols across equivalent devices.

Bar Top Referring Physicians — Volume

Who are the top 10 physicians sending us patients in this period?

These are your highest-priority retention relationships. Losing one top-10 referrer has an outsized revenue impact — flag them for the business development team immediately.

Bar Physician Loyalty — Unique Patient Reach

Which physicians refer the widest variety of distinct patients, rather than the same patients repeatedly?

High unique-patient count = broad community referrer. High volume with low unique count = chronic-care specialist. These are two different business profiles requiring different engagement strategies.

Stacked Bar Physician Modality Preference

What imaging mix does each physician order — how much CT vs MRI vs X-Ray?

A CT-heavy physician who rarely orders MRI is a cross-selling opportunity. Shifts in one modality from a key physician are advance warning of equipment demand changes.

Stacked Bar Study Status per Physician

Are studies from a specific physician mostly completing, or accumulating in a cancelled or pending state?

A physician with unusually high cancellation rates may have a scheduling friction problem. Targeted outreach can recover that revenue before the physician stops referring altogether.

Bar Overall Case Status Distribution

Across all studies, what is the split between Completed, Cancelled, In-Progress, and Pending?

A rising cancellation bar is an early warning of operational disruption. Investigate the cause before it impacts the monthly volume report delivered to management.

Patient Demographics
Bar Average Patient Age per Physician

Does a physician's patient panel skew younger or older than the department average?

Physicians with very young panels drive paediatric protocol and contrast-dose planning needs. Physicians with very old panels affect contrast-risk screening capacity. Both require different preparation.

Bar Table Average Patient Age per Procedure

What is the typical patient age for each procedure — and are any procedures being performed outside their expected demographic?

Screening mammography with an average age under 35 may indicate miscoding. Use the full searchable table for any procedure-level investigation — the chart shows top 20 by volume.

Rose Pie Gender Distribution

What is the male-to-female split across all studies in this period?

A significant or persistent skew may indicate a service gap — consistently low female volume is worth investigating against breast imaging capacity relative to the local population.

Area Line Age Group Distribution

How is the patient population distributed across age bands — Neonate, Child, Adolescent, Adult, Senior?

A large paediatric share drives the need for dedicated protocols, sedation capacity, and appropriately sized equipment — all requiring significant lead time to provision.

Churn Monitoring
Alert Cards Physician Churn Alerts

Which physicians sent fewer referrals this month compared to last month — and by how much?

Act on these immediately. A declining physician who is not re-engaged within 30 days is likely lost to a competitor. Each card shows the exact volume drop and percentage change so you can prioritise re-engagement calls.

An ER spike here directly drives staffing, contrast preparation, and porter logistics decisions — and may justify an extended-hours business case.

Area Line Monthly Volume Trend

Are we scanning more or fewer patients this month compared to previous months — and is there a seasonal pattern?

Summer dips, Ramadan shifts, and post-holiday surges are all visible here months in advance. Use them for proactive staffing adjustments rather than reactive scrambles.

Heatmap Modality × Patient Class

Which modality absorbs the most Emergency patient volume — and is one device the only option for ER cases?

A modality overwhelmingly used by ER patients is a critical bottleneck during emergencies. Use this to guide equipment prioritisation and redundancy planning.

Bar Average Age by Modality

What is the average patient age for each imaging modality — and does it match the expected clinical profile?

Mismatches between expected and actual age profiles for a modality may signal referral pathway issues or protocol misuse that is worth a clinical audit.

Stacked Bar Age Group × Procedure Distribution

Are our top procedures age-driven — for example, are head CTs concentrated in seniors?

Age-procedure correlations provide evidence for subspecialty investment. A procedure predominantly ordered in paediatric patients justifies a dedicated paediatric protocol team.

Stacked Bar Age Distribution per Modality

What percentage of each modality's patients are paediatric, adult, or senior?

A modality with 30% paediatric load requires different contrast doses, protocols, and staff training than one at 5%. Plan these differences proactively rather than case by case.

Rose Pie Gender Ratio

What is the male-to-female ratio across the entire patient population in this period?

Use as a baseline for equity reporting and breast imaging capacity planning relative to the actual female population share being served.

Multi-Axis Bar Age × Gender × Modality Cube

What is the volume of CT studies in adult females — or any other specific combination of age group, gender, and modality?

The most information-dense demographic chart on the platform. Use it for targeted health programme reporting or grant applications that require specific population segments in a single view.

KPI Cards Patient Return Rates (30 / 60 / 90 days)

What percentage of patients came back for a second visit within 30, 60, and 90 days of their first study?

Very high 30-day return rates combined with low satisfaction may indicate incomplete initial studies rather than genuine follow-up need — worth reviewing with the clinical lead.

Report 25

Operations & Performance

The operational performance hub. Tracks turnaround time (TAT), device utilisation, radiologist and technician productivity, and unread study backlogs — across multiple tabs in the same page.

Overview
KPI Row Global Summary KPIs

What are the headline numbers right now — utilisation %, total RVU, high-stress device count, and ER average TAT?

Check these first. Red numbers mean something needs attention before drilling into detail charts. Green numbers confirm the department is running normally today.

Bar Hourly Arrival Pattern

What time of day does the most volume arrive — and when are the quiet periods?

If peak hour is 10:00–12:00 and your second radiologist starts at 12:00, this chart is the data needed to justify shifting that schedule. Also reveals night-shift demand for on-call planning.

Donut Modality Volume Split

What percentage of total studies does each modality account for?

If CT is 60% of volume but only 20% of RVU, that equipment investment imbalance is a budget discussion point for the next planning cycle.

Turnaround Time (TAT)
Bar TAT per Patient Class

Are ER, Inpatient, and Outpatient studies being reported within their expected SLA windows?

ER patients need sub-60-minute TAT. Any bar exceeding that limit requires immediate investigation into the reporting queue for that specific care setting.

Bar Overlay Modality TAT — Infra tab

Which imaging modalities have structurally longer reporting delays — not just occasional outliers?

When mean and median diverge significantly for a modality, a few extreme cases are pulling the average — a different problem from systematic slowness, and requiring a different fix. Only modalities with ≥5 signed studies are shown.

Bar Best vs Worst AE by TAT

Which specific scanner has the worst average turnaround time compared to all other devices?

A device dramatically worse than its peers may have a worklist configuration issue, network latency, or a staffing problem on its floor — investigate that device specifically rather than the department overall.

Histogram TAT Distribution

Is TAT performance consistent across the department, or is there high variability — some fast, some very slow?

A bimodal shape (two peaks) reveals two distinct workflow streams — for example daytime reads and overnight reads — each needing its own SLA target and its own staffing solution.

Scatter Duration vs TAT Correlation

For each procedure, does the reporting delay grow in proportion to scan time — or is something specifically bottlenecking the reporting side?

Procedures far above the diagonal have long TATs relative to their scan time — the delay is in reporting, not scanning. Focus workflow improvement there rather than on scanner throughput.

Scatter RVU vs TAT Correlation

Are high-value studies being reported promptly, or are they waiting as long as low-value ones?

High-RVU studies with long TATs are the most critical improvement targets — revenue is being generated but delayed, risking referring physician satisfaction and repeat business.

Table TAT Outlier Table

Which specific studies experienced severe delays — more than double the department average TAT?

Each row is a candidate for root-cause analysis. Use the accession number and date to determine whether the delay was a worklist failure, a staffing gap, or a public holiday not accounted for in scheduling.

Backlog
KPI Stacked Bar Unread Study Aging — Workflow tab

How many studies have been waiting more than 24, 48, or 72 hours without a signed report?

The 72h+ bucket is a direct clinical risk. The modality breakdown shows exactly which imaging type is accumulating the backlog — enabling targeted radiologist reassignment before it becomes a patient safety incident.

KPI Bar Addendum Rate by Radiologist — Workflow tab

A high addendum rate is a quality indicator, not automatically a performance problem. Review it alongside case volume and complexity before any peer discussion — context determines whether it is a training issue or a complex case-mix issue.

KPI Bar Table Technician Performance & TAT — Technicians tab

How long does each technician take to complete exams — and are any exams suspiciously fast or suspiciously slow?

"Too short" flags (below 50% of expected duration) may mean skipped protocol steps. "Too long" flags surface bottlenecks — patient delays, equipment issues, or training gaps. The Flagged Exams table turns statistics into a reviewable case list with accession numbers.

KPI Donut Studies per Shift — Workflow tab

If Night shift is absorbing disproportionate volume while Morning is underutilised, adjust rosters with this data. Shift boundaries are configurable via the "Configure Shifts" button — set them to match your actual hospital schedule before interpreting results.

Devices
Matrix Table Device Utilisation & Revenue Matrix

Which devices are overloaded on specific days of the week — and which are chronically idle?

Chronically red cells on specific weekdays are schedule redistribution targets. Consistently grey cells (closed or no schedule) are decommissioning or redeployment candidates. Colours: green = normal, amber = >75%, red = >85%, grey = closed.

Efficiency
KPI Bar Scatter Efficiency Intelligence Hub — Efficiency Intel tab

Which modalities generate the most revenue per unit of capacity consumed — and which are burning capacity without proportionate output?

High utilisation with low RVU is a budget argument for protocol review or service mix adjustment. TAT outlier detection here uses a statistically sounder IQR-based threshold rather than the mean — more robust when a handful of extreme cases skew the average.

Patient Journey
Timeline Patient Journey — Full Study Lifecycle — Patient Journey tab

Search by Patient ID or Accession Number. Every milestone from Order Received to Final Report Signed is shown with the exact minutes between each step. This collapses a three-system investigation into one lookup — the primary root-cause tool for individual case delays.

Intelligence

Report Intelligence

Donut Treemap Normal / Abnormal Classification

What proportion of reports are classified Normal, Abnormal, or Critical — and does the rate vary by modality?

An unexpected shift in the abnormality rate for a specific modality may indicate over-templating ("normal" being applied without reading) or a genuine case-mix change worth escalating to clinical leadership.

KPI Table Critical Findings Log

Which reports contain critical findings — and is there an auditable record that they were identified and logged?

Use this as an accreditation evidence register and to verify that critical findings are being communicated to referring physicians. The keyword list is fully customisable — add facility-specific terms from the settings.

Treemap Report Term Treemap

What are the most frequently used clinical terms across all reports — and which report sections do they originate from?

This is the vocabulary fingerprint of your department. If "correlation is suggested" dominates the Conclusion section across 90% of reports, that is a templating problem diluting report clinical value.

Bar Section-Level Term Frequency

What vocabulary is being used separately in the Technique, Findings, and Conclusion sections?

Helps standardise reporting language across the department and identifies where boilerplate phrases are replacing meaningful clinical content in specific sections.

Table Bar Gap Analysis — Empty Sections per Radiologist

Which radiologists are routinely leaving report sections (Technique, Findings, Conclusion) empty or too short?

Structural completeness is an accreditation requirement. This gives you an objective rate — not a single incident — making peer review conversations constructive and data-backed rather than anecdotal.

Bar Physician Activity & Report Volume

How does each radiologist's report volume compare — and what is their classification rate (Normal vs Abnormal vs Critical)?

High volume with a consistently low abnormality rate, combined with a high section gap rate, suggests under-reporting of findings. Review alongside the Gap Analysis and Addendum Rate from Operations for a complete quality picture.

Cluster Severity Scoring & Cluster Analysis

Are there emerging diagnostic clusters — groups of reports sharing similar clinical terms more frequently than expected?

A sudden cluster of pulmonary embolism-related reports over two weeks that does not match referral pattern changes could indicate a local environmental risk factor worth escalating to clinical leadership.

Predictive Intelligence

Machine-learning forecasting applied to storage, study volume, device utilisation, and referral trends. Uses linear regression and standard deviation-based anomaly detection — all models run locally with no external API calls.

Line + Forecast Storage Forecast — Days to Capacity

At the current growth rate, when will PACS storage be full?

Storage procurement takes 8–12 weeks. The days-to-full KPI tells you whether you need to act now. Don't wait for a "disk full" error to appear — by then it is already a patient safety issue.

Line + Anomalies Volume Forecast & Anomaly Detection

Are study volumes growing, flat, or declining — and were there any statistically abnormal days recently?

Anomaly flags on specific dates are starting points for investigation. A volume spike on a public holiday is likely a data duplication issue. A sudden drop may be a worklist failure that went unnoticed at the time.

Line + Forecast Per-Device Utilisation Forecast

Which device will breach the 85% stress threshold first — and approximately when?

A device trending upward at 80% will breach the limit in a calculable number of weeks. Use the forecast to schedule maintenance pauses or add session capacity before the device becomes a patient-flow bottleneck.

Scatter Stress vs Output (Efficiency Frontier)

Is each device in the right quadrant — high utilisation with high revenue, or burning capacity without proportionate output?

Bottom-right (high stress, low RVU) devices are working hard for little revenue — a protocol review argument. Top-left (low stress, high RVU) devices have capacity that should be filled with high-value bookings.

Line + Forecast Table Physician Referral Forecast

Which physicians are trending up, which are stable, and which are declining — with a 3-month projection?

Declining physicians with negative year-over-year change are retention risks requiring immediate engagement. Growing physicians are relationships to invest in. The forecast gives business development a quantified, prioritised pipeline.

Copy this paragraph directly into your weekly operational briefing or management email — no reformatting needed. Generated from actual database values using deterministic templates, not an AI language model.

Report 27

Audit & Comparison

Validates data integrity between the RIS and PACS, and enables side-by-side comparison of two time periods to measure real growth or regression.

KPI Cards Audit KPIs — Match %, Orphans, Volume Delta

Are we doing more or less than this time last year — and is the underlying data reliable enough to trust that answer?

Check the RIS-PACS procedure code match rate first. If it is below 95%, the volume delta may be an artefact of data quality rather than real activity change. Never report a growth figure to leadership without confirming the match rate first.

Donut Procedure Code Reconciliation (RIS vs PACS)

What percentage of RIS orders have procedure codes that exactly match what PACS recorded?

A low match rate corrupts billing accuracy and all procedure-level analytics. Each mismatch type (code different / no PACS record / no order record) points to a different remediation target to bring to the IT team.

Area Line Hourly Load — Order Scheduling Pattern

When during the day are orders being placed in the RIS — and does that match when scans actually happen?

A front-desk order surge at 08:00 followed by a scanning surge at 11:00 reveals the scheduling-to-execution gap. Use this to manage worklist preparation and contrast pre-loading timing.

Bar Order Status Mix

How many orders in the RIS are Completed vs Cancelled vs still Active?

High cancellation volume may indicate a patient no-show problem or a scheduling inefficiency. A sustained rise in the cancellation bar is the trigger to investigate a targeted intervention programme.

Stacked Bar Demographics — Age × Gender

Has the demographic composition of patients changed between period A and period B?

A demographic shift between periods may explain a volume change rather than a referral trend. Confirm composition is stable before attributing a delta to business development activity.

Bar Modality Distribution by AE Device

Which devices received the most RIS orders — and does that match actual PACS device usage?

Discrepancies between RIS-ordered device and PACS-actual device reveal orders being rerouted without documentation — a billing accuracy risk that needs a protocol correction.

Report 29

Storage Audit

PACS storage consumption by modality, device, and procedure. Designed for IT infrastructure planning and protocol optimisation to control archive growth.

Bar Storage by Modality (GB)

Which imaging modality is consuming the most PACS archive storage in this period?

CT and MRI are disproportionate consumers due to multi-slice protocols. This quantifies the imbalance so tiered-storage decisions are evidence-based — move old CT to cold storage first.

Bar Device Storage Efficiency (Avg MB / Study)

Which scanner produces the largest files per study — and is that clinically justified by the protocol?

A device above 500 MB per study (shown in red) may have unnecessarily thin CT slices or over-scanned sequences. Fixing one protocol on one device can save tens of TB annually — bring the device name and procedure code to the medical physicist.

Alert Cards Storage Hog Alerts

Which specific procedure codes are generating files far above the expected size threshold?

Each alert card is a physics meeting agenda item. Bring the AE device name and procedure code — these are typically resolved in one protocol adjustment session and have outsized storage impact.

Table Protocol Density Inventory

Across all procedure-device combinations, which have the highest average file size and the highest total volume?

Sort by high volume × high MB/study to create a ranked optimisation target list. This is the document to bring to the PACS administrator to prioritise protocol review for maximum storage savings per intervention.

ETL

ETL Pipeline

KPI Run Log Pipeline Health Dashboard

Did last night's data sync complete successfully — and if not, which of the 8 phases failed?

Yes — click "Run Now" on this page. Alternatively, run docker exec -it rayd_service python app.py -m on the server. Use this after correcting a failed run or when fresh data is needed mid-day for an urgent management report.

Setup Availability & Response Time

Why is the AI assistant sometimes slow or unavailable?

The AI runs entirely on the server's CPU — no internet connection or external API is used. Response time is 5–15 seconds per question. Identical questions within 5 minutes are answered instantly from cache. If unavailable, check whether the llama-server service is running on the host machine.
